Welcome to the Accessible Password Study

Background

This study compares traditional text-based authentication methods (like PINs) with graphical authentication methods (like drawing patterns or selecting images). We are especially interested in understanding how these methods work for neurodivergent individuals.

This study is about your experience—there are no right or wrong answers. Please try your best and let us know if anything is unclear or difficult.

Instructions for the Session

You will complete three different authentication tasks using a website. Each task will follow the same structure:

  • Create your own password or pattern
  • Do a short unrelated activity
  • Use your password or pattern to log in
  • Complete a short survey reflecting on that experience

The tasks will include:

  • An text-based password login
  • A number-based PIN login
  • A pattern lock system
  • An image-based sequence login

The order of these tasks will be randomized for each participant. You are encouraged to think aloud during each task—this means saying your thoughts or feelings out loud as you go. This helps us understand your experience better. If anything is confusing or doesn’t work as expected, please let the facilitator know.
